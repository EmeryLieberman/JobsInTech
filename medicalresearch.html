Medical researchers are part of the backbone of our society. They research, study and help prevent disease outbreaks and predict trends, develop vaccines and manage public health crises. They lessen or eradicate the impact of health issues in our society. They can also travel to other areas of the world to study emerging or consistent health problems. Think maternal mortality, HIV/AIDS, the Zika virus, and COVID. Aside from world-ending health conflicts, they work on improving life for people, which can also mean the development of prosthetics and allergy-free medication.

Things like clinical trials allow for medicine and vaccines to be slowly introduced to the world. Collectively, these forms of health research have led to significant discoveries, the development of new therapies, and a remarkable improvement in health care and public health. If it weren't for medical researchers, the COVID-19 pandemic would have killed millions more globally. Technological advancements and life-saving treatments in the medical field are thanks to the front line workers that help improve our lives.
